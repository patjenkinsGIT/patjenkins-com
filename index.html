01 — About

Operator. Builder. Translator.

I've spent 17 years as a customer-facing infrastructure and security implementer — starting at Oracle on the datacenter floor (Dulles federal/civilian cloud hosting), then PCI data protection at Fiserv, firm-wide device identity at McKinsey, a 600K-cert hybrid estate at Elevance Health, and most recently advising a regional bank on Venafi architecture and certificate governance through CDW. Regulated environments, audit-driven shops, FedRAMP and PCI footprints — that's the world I came up in.

PKI and certificate management is the backbone of everything I do — machine identity, cryptographic trust, and the lifecycle automation that keeps large estates from falling over. The next chapter builds on the other half of my history: data center operations and hardware bring-up. I'm NVIDIA-certified in AI Infrastructure & Operations (NCA-AIIO) and focused on AI data center buildouts — the racks, fabrics, and identity plumbing that AI workloads run on. The common thread hasn't changed — translate deep technical work into something a customer, an executive, or a junior engineer can actually use.

What's changed is how I ship. I run a modern AI-augmented engineering stack — Claude as primary engineering and operations partner, Replit for iteration, self-hosted MCP servers on DigitalOcean for production tooling. I direct AI rather than write code from scratch, and that lets one operator do the work of a small team. This site, for what it's worth, was built that way.
